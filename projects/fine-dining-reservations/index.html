Analysis July 2025

Fine-Dining Reservation Times

Working as maître d' at a fine-dining restaurant, I suspected certain servers were consistently running over the 1h 45m reservation window, hurting both guest experience and revenue. I built the case from six months of real POS and reservation data to justify schedule changes and dining optimization.

Python Pandas Power BI
Background

The Problem

As the maître d' at a fine-dining restaurant, my least favorite part of the job is telling a guest their table isn't ready when it should have been 15 minutes ago. Over time I developed a sense of which servers in the restaurant tended to run past the time frame we give tables, and I needed data to back up my case.

In my administrative role I had access to both our POS system (Toast) and our reservation platform (OpenTable). I worked with my managers to pull it together and make the case properly.

Approach

How I Built It

We gathered six months of data from OpenTable and Toast from January 2nd through June 30th, 2025. OpenTable provided reservation records that detailed server assignments and timestamps from being seated to getting up. Toast provided more detailed sales data and an idea of when courses were sent.

All aggregation and cleaning was done in Python using Pandas. The main challenge was joining the two datasets: Toast and OpenTable don't share a common key, so I matched records on table number, party size, and time proximity. After cleaning, each row represented a single dining party with their server, seated time, vacated time, total duration, and revenue.

The dashboard was built in Power BI. The focus was dining time rather than revenue. I wanted to know what proportion of each server's tables stayed within the 1h 45m reservation window, and how that related to their sales figures.

Dashboard

The Report

The dashboard breaks down dining times by server, shift, and day of week. Key points include the percentage of tables within the reservation window per server, average duration by time slot, and a revenue-per-minute metric that determines who serves the most efficiently in duration and sales.

Revenue figures have been slightly altered in agreement with the restaurant owners before publishing this report.

Power BI dashboard: dining duration by server, shift efficiency, and revenue per minute. Revenue figures have been adjusted for publication.
Outcome

What Changed

The data shows clear, consistent differences between servers in how often they stayed within the reservation window.

The dashboard was enough to convince my managers of what I'd suspected. Since then, the usual server lineup for busier weekend shifts has been adjusted to prioritize the most efficient servers. Now that it feels much smoother, I'll return in a couple months with new data to show off the improvement.
